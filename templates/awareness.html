Common Types of Frauds

Phishing

Fraudsters trick you into revealing sensitive info via fake emails or websites.

Card Skimming

Hidden devices steal your card data at ATMs or gas stations.

Fake Investments

Promises of "guaranteed returns" to scam you into investing your money.

Account Takeover

Hackers steal login details to access and empty your accounts.

Real Fraud Case: The Story of Sarah Johnson

In 2024, Sarah Johnson, a small business owner from New York, received an urgent email that appeared to be from her bank. The email warned her about "suspicious activities" on her account and urged her to verify her credentials immediately through a provided link.

Trusting the email, Sarah clicked the link and entered her bank details. Within minutes, unauthorized transactions began draining her account.
